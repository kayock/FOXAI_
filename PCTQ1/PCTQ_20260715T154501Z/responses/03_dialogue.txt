Mara: This room is just a box of memories. Let’s not waste time with it.  
Ivo: [nervously adjusting his sleeve] Opening it feels like exhaling after holding your breath underwater.  
Mara: Your metaphors are worse than the silence in here. What’s inside matters more than your drama.  
Ivo: [steps closer to the door] Maybe the air inside has answers we haven’t asked for yet.  
Mara: Answers won’t fix the fact that we’re both avoiding what’s real.  
Ivo: Realness is fragile—like holding a moth in your hands. You’d drop it before holding it gently.  
Mara: That’s your way of saying you’d ruin everything with curiosity.  
Ivo: [softening slightly] Maybe curiosity isn’t the enemy. It’s just… untamed.  
Mara: Untamed things make messes. What if it’s better to leave the room closed?  
Ivo: [gazing at the door] Or what if it’s better to face what’s been waiting this long?  
Mara: You’re romanticizing a trap. Practicality keeps us safe.  
Ivo: Safety is a cage. Sometimes cages have locks that break on their own.  
Mara: [sighs] I’ll decide when you’re ready to stop talking in riddles.  
Ivo: [quietly] I’ll wait until you’re ready to see what’s on the other side.  
Mara: [turns away] We’ll leave it closed. Always safer that way.  
Ivo: [remains by the door, unmoving] Not if the door decides to open first.
