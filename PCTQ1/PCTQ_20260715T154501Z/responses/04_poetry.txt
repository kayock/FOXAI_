Small Machines of Mercy  

The chipped blue cup still holds yesterday’s tea,  
a quiet testament to warmth that once passed through.  
The sleeping radio hums a lullaby for the dust  
that settles softly on forgotten edges.  

Rain fills the glove like a secret held tight—  
not poured out, but kept,  
waiting for a hand to remember how to receive.  

Each image is a small machine,  
moving parts of tenderness  
that never stop, even in stillness.
