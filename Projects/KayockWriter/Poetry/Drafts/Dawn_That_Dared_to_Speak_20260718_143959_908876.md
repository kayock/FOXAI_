# Dawn That Dared to Speak

He stood where shadows used to fall,  
The void not empty, but alive—  
A blaze that swallowed breath, a thrall  
To light that burned without relief.  

The silence wasn’t quiet—  
It *sang*, sharp and searing,  
A hymn of endings, a reckoning,  
A fire that couldn’t be fearing.  

He reached through heat, through white,  
His hands met ash—no form, no name—  
A presence vast, yet void of might,  
That knew him before he came.  

And in that fierce, unyielding light,  
He felt his bones begin to break.  
The terror wasn’t in the night—  
But in the dawn that dared to speak.
