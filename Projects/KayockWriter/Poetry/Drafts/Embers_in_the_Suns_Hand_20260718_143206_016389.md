# Embers in the Sun’s Hand

The sun swells, burning through the sky’s thin veil,  
I walk where shadows crumble into flame.  
My bones ache from carrying endless weight,  
Each step through heat that’s searing, shame.  

I’ve loved in deserts, fought for borrowed breath,  
Yet still the sun claims what I’ve tried to keep.  
It warms the stones, but not the wounds beneath—  
My heart’s a ember in a world that sleeps.  

Let me rest where the light forgets to shine,  
Where darkness cradles what the sun can’t mend.  
I’ll let the heat collapse, let it define  
What love’s left when the world is too cold to bend.
