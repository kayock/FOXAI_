# Love's Unfinished Code

The hammer falls, the blueprint cracks,  
His hands tremble with each struck spark.  
Each nail misses, each word’s unspooled—  
A machine speaks, not his truth, not the field he’d molded.  

He builds for the future, but the future sleeps,  
His kids’ eyes watch, not the plans he keeps.  
The code hums cold, while his chest aches hot—  
A father’s work, left unfinished, shot.  

The screen glows bright, but it can’t recall  
The nights he spent to reach the wall.  
It won’t cradle them, soothe their cries,  
Nor hold his dreams—the ones that die.  

He’s chasing a ghost in a borrowed light,  
A life unmade in a shadowed fight.  
The clock’s a thief, and time’s a curse—  
The work won’t wait, but neither will he.  

He collapses, not from the build,  
But from the love that can’t be stilled.  
The machine answers, but it can’t feel  
The weight of hope that’s turned to steel.  

His hands go still, the screen goes dim—  
The work is done, but not the crime.  
For in this world where love runs thin,  
The greatest legacy can’t begin.
