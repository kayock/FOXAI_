# Mirror of Unspoken Storm

He stared into the abyss, his breath a whisper  
Of rage that carved the dark like thunder’s split  
The void answered not, yet still it listened—  
A silence heavy as the weight of worlds.  

He roared into the dark, his voice unbound,  
A storm that shook the stars, unanchored,  
But the void swallowed his words whole,  
Leaving him with the ache of unanswered rage.  

What if the void’s not emptiness, but mirror?  
What if it holds what we refuse to face—  
The truth that love and death are one,  
The lie that light can ever last?  

He turned away, his hands clenched tight,  
The anger still burning in his veins—  
Not for the void, but for the world  
That keeps pretending it doesn’t care.  

Let the void stay hollow. Let it roar.  
He’ll face it again—  
Not to answer, but to scream  
That he still *feels*, still *matters*.
