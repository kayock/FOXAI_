# No man is an island part 2

No man is an island, entire of itself,  
Every man is a piece of the continent,  
A part of the main. If a clod be washed away by the sea,  
Europe is the less. As well as if a promontory were.  
As well as if a manor of thy friend’s  
Or of thine own were: Any man’s death diminishes me,  
Because I am involved in mankind,  
And therefore never send to know for whom the bell tolls;  
It tolls for thee.  

Even in the hollow ache of loneliness,  
I feel the pulse of every soul in my veins.  
The world is woven—no thread torn without  
My own thread feeling frayed.  
So I reach out, not to fill the void,  
But to let the echoes remind me:  
You are still here.  
You are still here.  
You are still here.
