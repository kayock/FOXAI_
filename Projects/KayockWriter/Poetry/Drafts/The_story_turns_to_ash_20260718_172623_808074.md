# The story turns to ash

The candle sputters on the velvet chair,
A dying breath upon a painted floor,
Where shadows stretch their long, accusing hair
And whisper secrets to the cold and bare door.
I speak aloud to walls that do not hear,
To dust motes dancing in the moon's pale stream,
While ghosts of yesterday stand at the ear
Of silence, waiting for a single scream.

Remember when the windows wept with rain,
And thunder cracked the ceiling like a bone?
We built a castle from the garden's pain,
And called the storm our ancient, roaring throne.
The clock struck twelve, a sharp and jagged note,
That froze the blood within this haunted hall,
While you were mine, the world a hollow lot,
And every shadow held a stolen, stolen fall.

Now I recount the verses of our love,
But every sentence stings like glass and stone,
As if the very air is full of dove
That died before the winter's frost had grown.
The mirror cracks, a spider's web of light,
Reflecting faces that have long since fled,
Where shadows dance and turn to frantic fright
And whisper names the living dared not speak, then fled.

The bed is cold, the tapestry is torn,
The curtains hang like shrouds upon the frame,
While silence grows a heavy, iron morn,
And swallows up the echoes of our name.
I trace the rim of this forgotten cup,
Where wine was spilled to wash away the fear,
And I will never taste the liquid, or the cup,
But will remember how the wine made me fear.

The wind outside begins a mournful song,
A lullaby for children long since dead,
It rattles loose the loose, the heavy, strong
And turns the floorboards where the children tread.
My voice grows weak, the rhythm starts to break,
As if the very floor begins to turn to dust,
And I am left without a place to speak,
With nothing but the memory to trust.

The door creaks open, though no hand is there,
The wood groans deep like a beast in chains,
And pulls me back to this forgotten air,
To stand within the corridors of pain.
I say goodbye, I say the final word,
And watch the candle flame begin to fade,
While in the dark, a shadowed, spectral bird
Watches my soul within the empty shade.

Let the silence claim what it has earned to own,
Let memory haunt the rooms I used to keep,
And let the night become the only one
Who knows the things the living dare not sleep.
The story ends, the verses turn to ash,
The candle dies, the shadows take their stand,
And I am left to walk in this dark wash,
With only the ghosts of yesterday to land.
