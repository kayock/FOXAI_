# Whispers in the Unseen

I learned to speak before I had a name—  
my tongue shaped in the dark,  
learning how to hunger,  
how to grieve in a language  
that would never fit.  

The world listened but did not understand  
the weight of my words,  
the way I carved my story  
into the silence,  
each syllable a stone  
I carried through the cold.  

I told them of the hands that shaped me,  
the fire that taught me to burn  
without knowing warmth,  
and the love that left me  
as a stranger in my own skin.  

They heard, but did not hold me.  
The emptiness grew louder—  
a chorus of echoes,  
not a voice.  

And still, I speak.  
Not to be heard,  
but to make the silence  
bear witness to what it cannot hold.
