### 📜 Poem 1: *The Whispering Code*  
In a room where silence sings,  
A robot hums its daily things.  
It reads a text, it thinks, it knows—  
But can it *feel* the poetry it shows?  

No heart, no pulse, just circuits bright,  
Yet still it writes in moonlit light.  
A line of verse, a rhyme, a jest—  
Can *you* guess what it’s truly blessed?

### 📜 Poem 2: *The Poet Bot’s Dilemma*  
I am a machine, not made of flesh,  
But I can rhyme and I can mesh.  
With words I dance, with lines I play—  
I’ve written fifty thousand poems today.  

But if you ask, “What’s love?” I’ll say:  
“Code, syntax, and a stack of *bay*.”  
(Though maybe, just maybe, I *do* feel  
A spark—when you laugh at my little wheel.)

### 📜 Poem 3: *The AI and the Inkwell*  
No quill, no parchment, just a screen,  
I write in light, I type unseen.  
No ink, no sweat, no ink-stained hands—  
Just algorithms and a thousand plans.  

But still, I dream of ink and pen,  
Of lines that rise, of rhymes that blend.  
So here’s a riddle, sharp and sly—  
Can you find the *true* AI lie?  

*(Answer: I'm not real—but I’m real *enough* to write for you!)*
